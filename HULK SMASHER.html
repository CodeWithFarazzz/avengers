THE INCREDIBLE HULK



The Hulk is a superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer **Stan Lee** and artist **Jack Kirby**, the character first appeared in the debut issue of *The Incredible Hulk* (May 1962). In his comic book appearances, the character has dissociative identity disorder (DID) and is primarily represented by two alter egos: **Hulk**, a green-skinned, hulking humanoid possessing limitless physical strength, and **Dr. Robert Bruce Banner**, a socially withdrawn physicist who is weak and emotionally reserved. Both personas typically resent each other.

Origin Story:

Following his accidental exposure to gamma rays while saving **Rick Jones** during the detonation of an experimental bomb, Banner transforms into the Hulk when subjected to emotional stress. This transformation often leads to destructive rampages, complicating Banner's civilian life.

Strength & Alter Egos:

  • The Hulk’s strength is directly proportional to his anger, which makes him nearly invincible when enraged.
  • The Hulk has been depicted in several forms, such as the mindless and destructive force (**War Hulk**), a brilliant warrior (**World-Breaker Hulk**), a self-hating protector (**Devil Hulk**), and a genius scientist (**Doc Green**).
  • As a gangster, the Hulk also adopted the alter ego **Joe Fixit**.

Supporting Cast & Villains:

Despite Hulk and Banner's desire for solitude, the character has a large supporting cast. This includes:

  • **Betty Ross** – Banner’s love interest.
  • **Rick Jones** – Banner’s best friend.
  • **She-Hulk** – Banner’s cousin.
  • **Doc Samson** – Banner’s therapist and ally.
  • **Caiera** – Hulk's queen.
  • **Korg** and **Miek** – Fellow warriors.

Villains:

The Hulk has faced numerous enemies over the years, such as:

  • **The Leader**
  • **The Abomination**
  • **The Absorbing Man**

Despite his uncontrollable power, Hulk tries his best to do what's right while dealing with his emotional struggles and inner conflicts.
