SPIDER-MAN




Spider-Man is the secret identity of **Peter Benjamin Parker**, a teenager from New York City who was raised by his Aunt May and Uncle Ben after the tragic death of his parents. Initially, Spider-Man was depicted as an orphan struggling with the challenges of adolescence and young adulthood. Over time, he was given a rich array of supporting characters like Flash Thompson, J. Jonah Jameson, Harry Osborn, and romantic interests such as Gwen Stacy and Mary Jane Watson. Along with that, he faced formidable enemies like Doctor Octopus, Green Goblin, and Venom.

Origin Story:

In his origin story, Peter Parker gains his superhuman spider powers after being bitten by a radioactive spider. These powers include:

  • Superhuman strength, speed, agility, and reflexes.
  • Ability to cling to surfaces and ceilings like a spider.
  • Precognitive "spider-sense" to detect danger.
  • Wrist-mounted web-shooters to create and control webs for combat and travel.

Becoming Spider-Man:

Initially, Peter used his powers for personal gain. However, after his Uncle Ben is killed by a thief Peter could have stopped, he realizes the importance of responsibility. This moment leads him to embrace his role as a crime-fighter and protector, adopting the superhero persona of **Spider-Man**.
